The Shore Between

Essays on Memory and the Coast

What does it mean to belong to a place — and what happens when that place begins to disappear? In fourteen essays spanning two decades and a single coastline, Martin Pina explores the New England shore as both landscape and psychology: a terrain that holds the stories we have given it, long after we've moved on.

Part memoir, part meditation, The Shore Between is an investigation of how we carry the places that have formed us — and how they carry us back.

A Note from the Author

"I started writing these essays as a way of understanding a particular stretch of coastline. By the time I finished, I had written about time."

The Shore Between grew out of a decade of notebooks, winter walks, and early morning photographs — the work of someone trying to understand why certain places will not let us go.
