About

Martin
Pina

Author, essayist, and occasional photographer — based on the Massachusetts coast. Writing about memory, place, and the quiet stories we carry.

Get in Touch
Author Portrait

I grew up in the kind of place you only understand after you've left it. The Massachusetts coast has a way of holding on — not aggressively, not with drama, but with the patient insistence of tides and seasons. I spent years leaving and returning, and eventually I understood that the leaving and returning was itself the subject I had been trying to write.

My writing sits at the intersection of memoir and essay, landscape and interior. I am interested in the places that shape us without our permission — the harbor you grew up near, the road you drove too many times, the window you looked out of during difficult years. How these places accumulate in us. What we do with them.

My debut collection, The Shore Between, was published in 2026. It gathers fourteen essays written over more than a decade, exploring the New England coastline as both physical terrain and psychological landscape. The book took a long time to become itself, which is another way of saying I had to learn to trust the work I was actually doing rather than the work I thought I was supposed to be doing.

I also photograph, mostly landscapes and coastal light — the same preoccupations as the writing, approached from a different angle. A camera forces a different kind of attention. You cannot revise a photograph the way you revise a sentence. The image is committed to its moment in a way that I find both limiting and clarifying.

Every Wednesday I publish a new essay on this blog. Some are polished. Some are in process. All of them are attempts to think through something I haven't quite understood yet. That's what the writing is for.
